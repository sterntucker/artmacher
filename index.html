About the Artist

A life lived in color.

Tomi Nonenmacher-Stern is a painter and muralist whose work has been shaped by more than four decades of looking closely at the Hudson Valley — its ridgelines, its rivers, the way late afternoon light falls across a stone wall. A graduate of SUNY New Paltz, she has spent her career rooted in the same landscape that fills her canvases.

For over thirty years she taught art in the public schools, mentoring generations of young artists and earning multiple awards from the New York State Art Teachers Association (NYSATA) for her contributions to arts education. Her own practice deepened alongside her teaching — oils, acrylics, and mixed media works that move easily between intimate portraiture, atmospheric landscape, and large-scale public murals.

Today she is the official artist for the Survival of the Shawangunks Triathlon, an honor that brings together her two great loves: the mountains she grew up beside, and the discipline of putting brush to canvas. She accepts private commissions and continues to paint, daily, from her studio in New Paltz.
